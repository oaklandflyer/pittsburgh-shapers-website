How a Night Runs

THE EVENING IS THE MENU

Three parts, in order, every time. The format is fixed so the food and the people can change.

First Course

Arrive and Sit Down

No name tags at the door and no working the room. Assigned seats, mixed tables, and a first course already on the way.

Second Course

Someone Tells You Where It Came From

The owner, the cook, or a family member takes fifteen minutes between courses. Not a lecture. What the dish is, who made it first, and how it got to Pittsburgh.

Third Course

Questions, Then Dessert

Open floor while the last plates come out. Most nights this runs longer than the talk did.

For Restaurants & Partners

FILL YOUR SLOWEST NIGHT WITH PEOPLE WHO CAME TO LISTEN

CulturePGH books a private room or a closed section on a weeknight, brings a full table, and puts your story in front of it. You cook what you actually want to serve.

What We Ask

  • A weeknight, a room, and 25 to 30 covers
  • A set menu you choose, priced per head
  • Fifteen minutes from you or your family between courses
  • A headcount confirmation 48 hours out

What You Get

  • A booked room on a night that is usually quiet, paid at the table
  • Photography from the night, yours to use
  • A written feature on this site and across Global Shapers channels
  • A room of guests who leave knowing your name

One of the project leads replies within a few days. No obligation on a first call.

Thank you. A CulturePGH lead will reach out within a few days to talk through dates and menus.
